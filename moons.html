Earth Moon(s)

Earth's moon! It's been around for a very long time; almost the entirety of Earth's existence too. Some other facts are:

  • 1. Formed by a collison between the Earth and Theia, a Mars-sized object from space. That collison led to material flying, and the moon is one of the pieces
  • 2. It's only called "The Moon" because no one knew other moons existed until 1610 when Galileo dicovered 4 Jupiter moons
  • 3. Only one side of the Moon is shown to the Earth at all times

Earth's second Moon...except not really. It's a temporary mini-moon that got caught by our gravitational pull Sept. 29, 2024. Some other facts are:

  • 1. The "mini-moon" is actually the tiny asteroid named "2024 PT5"
  • 2. Lasted until November 25, 2024 at 15:43 UTC
  • 3. Belongs to the Arjuna asteroid belt, a belt that orbits the Sun 93 million miles away

Mars Moons

Deimos is the smaller moon of Mars, being 9 by 7 by 6.8 miles in size. Some other facts are:

  • 1. It orbits Mars every 30 hours
  • 2. It's much farther away from Mars, about 14,580 miles away
  • 3. It takes about 30.3 hours for it to orbit Mars

Since Deimos is the smaller moon, Phobos is clearly the bigger one, being 17 x 14 x 11 miles in size. Some other facts about Phobos are:

  • 1. It orbits Mars 3 times a Earth day
  • 2. The big crater on the moon is called the Stickney Crater, named after the wife of the man who doscovered the moon, aka Chloe Angeline Stickney Hall
  • 3. It's ~3,700 miles from Mars, which is almost the length of China from North to south (~3,400 miles)

Jupiter Moons

Ganymede, known for being the biggest moon that Jupiter holds and the biggest moon in the solar system, being larger than Mercury. Some other facts are

  • 1. Despite having old lava flows, its tempature ranges from -297 to -171 degrees F
  • 2. It has its own magnetic field
  • 3. Supposedly has more water stored than Earth has, and possibly in layers too

Callisto has always been considered a "Dead Moon" for its inactive geological activity. However, data from the Galileo spacecraft during the 1990s suggests there actually might be a ocean under the surface. Some other facts are:

  • 1. It's the most heavily cratered space object in the solar system
  • 2. 3rd largest moon in the solar system
  • 3. Due to there possibly being a ocean deep into Callisto, the moon may be habitable for life

Io is, by far, one of the most active moons, with so much volcanic activity that it practically changed the way it looks entirely. Some other facts are:

  • 1. Io's volcanic eruptions that they are seen with large telescopes on Earth
  • 2. Io is tidally locked just like Earth's natural moon
  • 3. Has the strongest surface gravity due to the volcanos

Saturn moons

Titan is Saturn's largest moon, and despite it's golden hue, which is just its atmosphere, it's a very icy moon. Some other facts are:

  • 1. Is larger than the planet Mercury and almost as big as Ganymede
  • 2. Its surface is one of the most Earthlike places, ignoring the extreme cold
  • 3. Is the only moon in the solar system with a thick atmosphere

Hyperion is a irregularly shaped moon, being the largest irregularly shaped moon Saturn has. Some other facts are:

  • 1. Has a sponge like surface with so many craters, including a 200 meter wide crater
  • 2. Has a eccentric orbit, meaning that its orbit is unpredictable and more chaotic
  • 3. 3 people are credited with the discovery of Hyperion in 1848, with their names being: William Lassell, William Cranch Bond, and George Phillips
