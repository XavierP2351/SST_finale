This is Bennu, also known as 101955 Bennu or 1999 RQ36! It's a near-Earth asteroid that was discovered in 1999 by the Lincoln Near-Earth Asteroid Research team. Some other facts are:

  • 1. NASA's OSIRIS-REx mission arrived at Bennu on December 3, 2018
  • 2. Considered a ancient relic of the "early days" of our solar system, with scientists thinking it was already made 10 million years after the formation of our solar system
  • 3. Then particles that make up the surface are so loosely packed that if someone were to step on Bennu, they would sink into the asteroid

This is Ceres, the first discovered asteroid ever in 1801 by Giuseppe Piazzi! It's also the largest object in the asteroid belt. Some other facts are:

  • 1. Was considered a asteroid before reclassified as a dwarf planet in 2006
  • 2. NASA's first true "interplanetary spaceship" landed on Ceres in 2015, being the first dwarf planet to ever receive a visit from a spacecraft
  • 3. its day is about 9 hours long, being one of the shortest days in the solar system

Pallas is the 3rd largest asteroid, found in between Mars and Jupiter. Some other facts are:

  • 1. It's denser than Ceres yet less dense than Vesta
  • 2. Its day is about 7.8 hours
  • 3. Holds a theory that Pallas could be a "remnant protoplanet" could've been a planet if under different conditions
